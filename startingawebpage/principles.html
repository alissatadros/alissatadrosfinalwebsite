11 Principles of Design (and How To Use Them)

Learn the core principles of design and how to apply them to your projects. With these principles, your designs will be visually appealing and user-friendly.

The 11 Principles of Design

  1. Hierarchy
  2. Balance
  3. Alignment
  4. Emphasis
  5. Proportion
  6. Movement
  7. Negative Space
  8. Contrast
  9. Repetition
  10. Variety
  11. Unity

1. Hierarchy

Use size, color, and typography to guide users through content. Important elements should stand out first, second, and so on.

2. Balance

Balance can be symmetrical or asymmetrical. Symmetry creates stability, asymmetry uses contrasting elements with equal visual weight.

3. Alignment

Proper alignment creates a sense of order. Align text and graphics consistently to avoid a messy layout.

4. Emphasis

Draw attention to focal points like headlines, images, or CTAs using size, color, contrast, or other techniques.

5. Proportion

Proportion is the relative size of elements. Larger elements attract attention, smaller elements recede. Use this to highlight key content.

7. Negative Space

Also called white space, it gives elements room to breathe and improves readability and visual hierarchy.

8. Contrast

Contrast can be in color, size, or shape. It helps elements stand out and improves accessibility.

9. Repetition

Repetition creates consistency, rhythm, and unity. Repeat visual elements like colors, shapes, or icons to strengthen your design.

10. Variety

Variety adds interest and prevents monotony. Introduce unique or unexpected elements while keeping overall harmony.

11. Unity

Unity gives a sense of cohesion. Achieve it through proximity, alignment, and consistent use of repeated elements.

Applying the Principles of Design

Use these principles to create cohesive, visually appealing, and user-friendly designs. Disregard them only if you understand the reasoning behind each.
